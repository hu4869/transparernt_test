Postdoc Position at the University of North Carolina at Charlotte

Applications are invited for one postdoc position to undertake research in the emerging area of “health informatics” at the department of Software and Information Systems, the University of North Carolina at Charlotte.

Health informatics (sometimes called medical informatics, clinical informatics, or biomedical informatics) is the study of information and computation in health. Researchers in this field study and manage information, study behavior related to decisions, and develop computational methods and use them to generate knowledge.

More specifically the successful candidate will conduct research by applying state-of-art data mining, text mining and image processing approaches to social network, health tracking, and clinical data to address an interesting real world problem.

Basic qualifications:

  • A PhD degree in computer science, informatics or related discipline such as engineering, mathematics, and physics with strong computational skills.
  • Practical programming, database, and data mining related skills are required.
  • Self motivated and goal oriented.
  • Strong publication record.
  • Available to start now.
